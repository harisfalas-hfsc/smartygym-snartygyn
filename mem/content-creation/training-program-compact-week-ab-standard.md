name: Training Program Compact Week A/B Standard
description: Training programs must use compact Week A/B workout templates with progression rules, not a new workout for every week.
type: feature
---
Training programs must be compact repeat-and-progress plans.

Rules:
- Do not generate Week 1, Week 2, Week 3, etc. as separate full workout blocks.
- Generate Program Goal, Program Instructions, Program Progression, Week A Template, and Week B Template only.
- Week A and Week B contain full daily workout templates with Soft Tissue Preparation, Activation/Warm-Up, Main Workout, Finisher, and Cool Down.
- Formatting is mandatory: every major bold header starts after one empty paragraph, including Program Instructions, Program Progression, Week templates, Objective, each Day, Soft Tissue, Activation, Main Workout, Finisher, Cool Down, Recovery, and Rest.
- Program length comes from progression instructions, not from listing every calendar week.
- Example: 6 weeks = Week 1 Week A, Week 2 Week A + progression, Week 3 Week B, Week 4 Week B + progression, Week 5 Week B + advanced progression, Week 6 Week B peak.
- Do not create endless scrolling schedules such as 6 weeks × 5 days or 12 weeks × 6 days.
- Category reasoning is mandatory before exercise selection: first determine Program Category, User Level, and Equipment Type, then ask "What adaptation am I trying to create?" and only select exercises that directly create that adaptation.
- Never randomly choose exercises from the exercise database. Training intent always comes before exercise selection.
- Remove skill exercises from general programs: Muscle Up, Full Maltese, Iron Cross, Planche, One Arm Push Up, Handstand Push Up, Front Lever, Back Lever, Human Flag, and similar gymnastics skills. Pistol Squat is Advanced-only and only when the category genuinely supports it.
- Cardio Endurance: improve aerobic capacity, stamina, and sustained work. Prefer walking, running, jogging, step-ups, jump rope, mountain climbers, jumping jacks, high knees, burpees, bear crawls, squats, reverse lunges, push-ups, skaters, fast feet. Avoid sissy squat, elite skills, and heavy strength emphasis.
- Weight Loss: maximize caloric expenditure, continuous movement, work capacity, and elevated heart rate. Prefer squats, lunges, push-ups, step-ups, mountain climbers, jumping jacks, burpees, high knees, bear crawls, dead bug, glute bridge, plank. Avoid heavy strength protocols, long rests, one-arm push-up, planche, muscle-up, and complex skills.
- Functional Strength: practical strength, force production, and movement quality. Prefer sets/reps, strength circuits, strength finishers, presses, pulls, rows, hinges, squats, carries, split squats, planks, pull-ups/chin-ups/dips when equipment and level allow. Avoid long cardio circuits and endless AMRAPs.
- Muscle Hypertrophy: muscle size through mechanical tension and volume. Prefer 6–15 reps, traditional hypertrophy, compound lifts, accessories, presses, rows, RDLs, goblet squats, split squats, shoulder press, lat pulldown, leg press. Avoid Tabata, EMOM, and long cardio circuits.
- Low Back Pain: pain reduction, spinal stability, movement quality, and core control. Sets/reps only. Prefer dead bug, bird dog, McGill curl-up, glute bridge, Pallof press, side plank, cat-camel, hip mobility, breathing, core stability. Never use circuits, AMRAP, EMOM, Tabata, HIIT, burpees, jumps, sprints, or box jumps.
- Mobility & Stability: mobility, joint control, balance, and movement quality. Sets/reps/holds only. Prefer World's Greatest Stretch, 90/90 hip mobility, thoracic rotation, deep squat hold, single-leg balance, bird dog, dead bug, hip airplane, shoulder and ankle mobility. Never use AMRAP, EMOM, circuits, Tabata, HIIT, burpees, sprints, jumps, or metabolic circuits.
